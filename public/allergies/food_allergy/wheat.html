Allergy to wheat products

Wheat is the most allergenic of all cereals. IgM antibodies have been found in many components of wheat kernels, albumin, globulin, gliadin, wheat germ agglutinin, a concanavalin A- purified glycoprotein, and a trypsin inhibitor. Wheat is the most rich in gluten, with the other grains containing a lesser mixture of gluten and gliadin.

Buckwheat is not a member of the grass family and is thus not true cereal. The grain may be used for human food in various forms from pancake flour to buckwheat noodles and baby foods.

For the wheat- hypersensitive individual, products made from oats, rice,rye,barley or corn or specialty foods made for gluten-sensitive individuals generally may be used instead of wheat. However, cross- reaction, although unusual, may occur between wheat, barley,rye, maize and rice.

Coeliac disease is a disorder of the small intestine, caused by a reaction to a gluten protein appearing in wheat, rye, oats, spelt and barley. Symptoms include chronic diarrhea and fatigue. Note that, while triggered by wheat, the disease is not the same as a wheat allergy. Coeliac disease- also called coeliac sprue- is determined with blood testing. A gluten-free diet remains the only defense against this condition.
