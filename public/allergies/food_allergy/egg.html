What is egg allergy?

Egg allergy is one of the most common food allergies in children, second only to milk allergy. As many as 2 percent of children are allergic to eggs. Fortunately, studies show that about 70 percent of children with an egg allergy will outgrow the condition by age 16.

What causes egg allergy?

Egg allergies are caused by an immune system over reaction. The immune system mistakenly identifies certain egg proteins as harmful. When you or your child comes in contact with egg proteins, immune system cells [antibodies] recognize them and signal the immune system to release histamine and other chemicals that cause allergic signs and symptoms.

Both egg yolks and egg whites contain proteins that can cause allergies, but allergy to egg whites is most common. It is possible for breast-fed infants to have an allergic reaction to egg proteins in breast milk if the mother consumes eggs.

Although ovalbumin, ovomucoid, and ovotransferrin have been identified as the major allergens in egg white, 0ther unnamed allergens of lesser importance have been identified. These allergens are also present in egg yolk but in lesser quantities. This is important because components of egg may be individually used for specific actions in food preparation. For example, hen's egg lysozyme is used as a preservative in food; and in some countries, notably Japan and Switzerland,lysozyme is used in medications. Individuals sensitive to hen's egg have been shown to be allergic to lysozyme produced from hen's egg.

Although rare, avian proteins can induce egg allergy in susceptible individuals. It has been suggested that duck egg be substituted for hen's egg in egg- sensitive individuals. These individuals are able to tolerate cooked chicken.

What are the symptoms of egg allergy?

Mild

  • The most common egg allergy reaction are skin inflammation or hives.
  • Allergic nasal inflammation [allergic rhinitis]
  • Digestive symptoms, such as cramps, nausea and vomiting
  • Asthma signs and symptoms such as coughing, chest tightness or shortness of breath.

Severe

Life threatening emergency

  • Requires an immediate adrenaline injection and a visit to the hospital
  • Constriction of airways. including a swollen throat or a lump in your throat that makes it difficult to breathe
  • Abdominal pain and cramping
  • Rapid Pulse
  • Shock, with a severe drop in blood pressure felt as dizziness, light headedness or loss of consciousness.

What are the risk factors?

Certain factors may increase the risk of developing an egg allergy.

  • Atopic dermatitis
  • Family history
  • Age

How is it diagnosed ?

Your doctor will start with a medical history and examination. Your doctor may also recommend one or more of the following tests.

  • Skin prick test.
  • Food challenge
  • Food tracking or elimination diet
  • Blood and skin tests are often used along with food challenges and diet changes.

How is egg allergy treated ?

Antihistamines may help to relieve mild symptoms of egg allergy, such as itching. In addition, your allergist may prescribe adrenaline injection, to be taken in the event there are symptoms of anaphylaxis a potentially fatal reaction that includes shortness of breath, swelling of the throat and dizziness from a sudden drop in blood pressure. An injection should be kept with you at all times and used as soon as symptoms start to appear.

Can a vaccination cause egg allergy?

Some vaccines contain egg proteins. In some people,these vaccines pose a risk of triggering an allergic reaction.

  • Flu (influenza) vaccines
  • Yellow fever vaccine
  • Rabies vaccines

For an Egg - Free Diet

Avoid foods that contain eggs or any of these ingredients

  • albumin(also spelled albumen)
  • mayonnaise
  • egg (dried, powdered, solids, white, yolk)
  • meringue(meringue powder)
  • eggnog
  • surimi
  • globulin
  • vitellin
  • livetin
  • words starting with "ovo" or " ova"(such as ovalbumin)
  • lysozyme

Egg is sometimes found in the following

  • baked foods
  • lecithin
  • breaded items
  • marzipan
  • drink foam (alcoholic, speciality coffee)
  • marshmallows
  • egg substitutes
  • meatloaf or meatballs
  • egg substitutes
  • fried rice
  • nougat
  • ice cream
  • pasta

Labels That hides eggs

List of products that can contain eggs

Individuals with egg allergy should also avoid eggs from Duck, Turkey, Goose, Quail, etc.,as these are known to be cross- reactive with chicken egg.
